What is a Gallbladder?

Your gallbladder is a small, pear-shaped organ that sits just under your liver on the right side of your belly. It's about the size of a small pear and works like a storage tank for a special liquid called bile that helps your body digest food!

Location Under the liver
Size About 4 inches long

What Does the Gallbladder Do?

The gallbladder has one super important job - to help digest fats! Here's how it works:

1

Stores Bile

Your liver makes bile all the time. The gallbladder stores this bile until it's needed!

2

Releases When Needed

When you eat fatty foods, your gallbladder squeezes out bile into your small intestine!

3

Breaks Down Fats

Bile helps break down fats into tiny pieces so your body can use them for energy!

Cool Gallbladder Facts!

Gallstones

Sometimes hard stones form in the gallbladder. These are called gallstones and can cause pain if they block the bile duct.

Live Without It

People can live without a gallbladder! If it's removed, the liver sends bile directly to the small intestine.

Animal Gallbladders

Most vertebrates have gallbladders. Even whales and dolphins have them to help digest their fishy meals!

Remember to eat healthy fats to keep your gallbladder happy!
